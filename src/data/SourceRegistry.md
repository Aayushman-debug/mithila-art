# Mithila Art - Source Registry

This document catalogs the authoritative sources used to populate the historically accurate information and imagery on the Mithila Art website, ensuring all content is verified and properly attributed.

## 1. Information Sources

### General History & Evolution of Madhubani Painting
*   **Government of Bihar (District Madhubani):** [Official District Portal](https://madhubani.nic.in/en/) - Used for verifying the geographical and historical origins (Janakpur legend).
*   **Ministry of Textiles (Handicrafts):** [Handicrafts Portal](http://handicrafts.nic.in/) - Details regarding the 1960s drought transition and the All India Handicrafts Board's intervention.
*   **GI Registry India:** Used to verify the Geographical Indication (GI) tag status and defined regional styles.

### Styles & Symbolism
*   **Indigo Arts Gallery Archive:** [Mithila Painting Context](https://www.indigoarts.com/) - Provided context on the 5 styles (Bharni, Kachni, Godna, Tantrik, Kohbar) and motif symbolism (Fish, Peacock, Lotus, Bamboo).

## 2. Artist Biographies & Image Attributions

### Sita Devi (1914–2005)
*   **Biography Source:** SaffronArt Artist Profile & Folkartopedia. Mentions her Padma Shri (1981) and transition of Bharni style to paper.
*   **Image Attribution:** Historical archive (Often associated with Edouard Boubat's 1970 portrait). For website purposes, we link to standard archival photos of her at work, attributed to the Ministry of Textiles / National Crafts Museum archives.

### Ganga Devi (1928–1991)
*   **Biography Source:** Asia Art Archive & SaffronArt. Detailed her mastery of the Kachni style, her "America Series", and her Padma Shri (1984).
*   **Image Attribution:** Archival photographs of her painting the Kohbar Ghar mural at the National Crafts Museum, New Delhi.

### Mahasundari Devi (1922–2013)
*   **Biography Source:** Wikipedia (verified against Government of India Padma Awards registry 2011).
*   **Image Attribution:** [Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9b/Devi6.JPG) - Image from her later years.

### Jagdamba Devi (1901–1984)
*   **Biography Source:** Folkartopedia. Verified her as the first Madhubani artist to receive the National Award (1970) and Padma Shri (1975).
*   **Image Attribution:** Archival photo courtesy of Upendra Maharathi Shilp Anusandhan Sansthan.

### Baua Devi (b. ~1940s)
*   **Biography Source:** Centre Pompidou archives (*Magiciens de la Terre* exhibition 1989) and Padma Awards registry (2017).
*   **Image Attribution:** [Wikimedia Commons (GODL-India)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/25/The_President%2C_Shri_Pranab_Mukherjee_presenting_the_Padma_Shri_Award_to_Smt._Baoa_Devi%2C_at_a_Civil_Investiture_Ceremony%2C_at_Rashtrapati_Bhavan%2C_in_New_Delhi_on_March_30%2C_2017.jpg) - Receiving Padma Shri from President Pranab Mukherjee.

### Dulari Devi (b. 1968)
*   **Biography Source:** *Following My Paintbrush* (Autobiography) and Padma Awards registry (2021).
*   **Image Attribution:** Archival photo, often provided by the Mithila Art Institute or Ojas Art.
